Threat Alert · Week 1
How AI Is Quietly Building a Profile on You Right Now
Every search, every click, every pause — AI is stitching together a detailed profile of who you are.

You do not have to do anything suspicious for AI to build a profile on you. Every interaction — every search, product page, video pause — is a data point being collected and sold.

How it gets built

Data brokers scrape your name, address, relatives, social media and court records. AI then predicts your income, health, politics and purchase intent from your behaviour.

What to do now

  • Search your name on Google — that is your public profile
  • Use DeleteMe to request removal from data broker databases
  • Use NordVPN to stop your ISP selling your browsing history
  • Switch to ProtonMail so emails are not scanned for targeting
How-To · Week 2
5 Settings on Your Phone Leaking Your Location Right Now
Your phone broadcasts your location to dozens of apps even when GPS is off. Here is how to stop it.

Turning off GPS does not stop tracking. Your phone triangulates position using WiFi, Bluetooth, cell towers and barometric pressure. Fix these five settings today.

The 5 settings

  • App location permissions — set all non-essential apps to Never
  • WiFi scanning — disable in Location settings even when WiFi is off
  • Bluetooth scanning — disable in Location settings
  • Google Location History / Apple Significant Locations — turn off and delete history
  • Ad ID — delete your Advertising ID on Android, disable tracking on iPhone
Deep Dive · Week 3
Facial Recognition Is in More Places Than You Think
Supermarkets, stadiums, schools. It is being deployed faster than any regulation can keep up.

Before you pick up a basket in some supermarkets, a camera has already matched your face against a database. Here is where facial recognition is deployed and how to make yourself harder to identify.

Where it is right now

  • Major retail chains and supermarkets
  • Sports stadiums and music venues
  • Airports and border crossings
  • Police departments in 30+ US states
  • Some schools and university campuses

What you can do

IR-blocking glasses like Reflectacles disrupt the infrared sensors most facial recognition cameras rely on while looking completely normal. The goal is not invisibility — it is raising the cost of surveillance high enough that you become not worth the effort.
